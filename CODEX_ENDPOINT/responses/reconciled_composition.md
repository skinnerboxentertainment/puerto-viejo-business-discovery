## 3. Composition Blueprint

Use a vertical portrait format, approximately **2:3 aspect ratio**, structured like a faux-antique illustrated book page with a glossy comic-cover finish.

### Top Header Zone

Create a formal printed title-card band at the top:

**"The Trickster, Upon Receiving a Most Cursed Remark"**

Include:

- Thin ornamental rule near the top edge
- Centered gothic-Victorian flourish
- Decorative serif title
- A second divider beneath the title
- Slightly aged letterpress irregularity, but polished enough to feel like a premium comic variant cover

### Main Illustration Zone

Below the header, stage a dense enchanted forest plate.

Keep the original compositional anchors:

- Upper-left/upper-center: Velvet Moon Imp lounging across a thick gnarled branch
- Lower-right: adult Crimson Card Trickster standing on the path, looking upward
- Center: winding forest path receding into a dark moonlit tunnel
- Lower-left: oversized poisonous mushrooms and a hidden eye-like shadow creature
- Left-mid area: a hanging clock pendant or broken pocket watch as a dark fairy-tale time symbol
- Right side: tall black roses, thorned foxglove shapes, and crimson petals echoing the heroine

### Spatial Depth

Preserve the three-plane structure:

**Foreground:**
Oversized red-and-black mushrooms, thorn vines, scattered playing cards, one hidden glowing eye beneath mushroom caps.

**Midground:**
Heroine on the lower-right path, imp on branch above, hanging clock pendant, twisted tree trunk framing the left side.

**Background:**
Moonlit forest tunnel, gothic arch-like tree shapes, mist, distant thorn gates, faint magenta magical glow.

### Visual Hierarchy

Guide the viewer in this order:

1. Gothic title at top
2. Large speech bubble from the imp
3. Velvet Moon Imp on the branch
4. Heroine's face, bodice, and raised hand
5. Smaller heroine speech bubble
6. Props and dark fairy-tale details

The speech bubbles remain the cleanest pale shapes in the image, cutting through the dense forest and preserving the original joke timing.

## 6. Joke / Speech Bubble Structure

Preserve the original two-bubble comedic rhythm.

### Imp's Bubble

Large cream speech bubble in the upper-left/center, above the branch, tail pointing toward the Velvet Moon Imp.

Text:

**"Every heart is cursed in these woods."**

This is broad, theatrical, and ominous, like a dark fairy-tale proverb.

### Heroine's Bubble

Smaller cream speech bubble at right-center, above the heroine, tail pointing toward her mouth.

Text:

This keeps the original insecure fragment structure of "at me?" while adapting it to the new heart/card/cursed-woods motif.

### Title

**"The Trickster, Upon Receiving a Most Cursed Remark"**

The title remains mock-literary and overly formal, heightening the mismatch between gothic grandeur and the heroine's tiny personal reaction.
